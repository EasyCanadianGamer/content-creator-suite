# Thumbnail & Title Optimizer

Produces high-CTR titles, A/B options with analysis, and detailed thumbnail design briefs for any platform.

---

## Step 1: Gather Info

Before generating, confirm:

| Input | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Topic / video summary** | What the content is about |
| **Platform** | YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter/X |
| **Target audience** | Who you're trying to get to click |
| **Tone** | Educational, entertaining, controversial, inspirational |
| **Keywords** | Any SEO terms the user wants to hit (YouTube especially) |

---

## Step 2: Generate Titles

Always produce **5 title options** across different psychological triggers:

### The 5 Title Archetypes

1. **Curiosity Gap** — Leave something unanswered that makes them need to click
   - "The One Thing Nobody Tells You About [X]"
   - "I Tried [X] for 30 Days — Here's What Happened"

2. **Specificity / Number** — Concrete numbers build trust and set expectations
   - "7 Ways to [Benefit] in [Timeframe]"
   - "How I Made $X in X Days Doing [X]"

3. **Controversy / Hot Take** — Challenge assumptions
   - "Why [Common Belief] Is Actually Wrong"
   - "Stop Doing [X] — Do This Instead"

4. **Direct Benefit / Outcome** — Tell them exactly what they'll get
   - "How to [Achieve Outcome] Without [Common Obstacle]"
   - "The Easiest Way to [Desired Result]"

5. **Personal Story / Journey** — Human and relatable
   - "I Almost Quit [X] — Until I Found This"
   - "What [X Years] of [X] Taught Me"

---

## Step 3: Title Scoring

Score each title on these 5 factors (1–10 each):

| Factor | What it measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Curiosity** | Does it open a loop the viewer needs to close? |
| **Clarity** | Is it immediately obvious what the video is about? |
| **Benefit** | Does the viewer know what they'll get? |
| **Emotion** | Does it trigger a feeling (excitement, fear, curiosity)? |
| **SEO** | Does it include searchable keywords naturally? |

Show scores in a table. Recommend the best title with a brief explanation.

---

## Step 4: Platform-Specific Title Rules

### YouTube
- Ideal length: 50–70 characters
- Front-load the most important keyword
- Avoid clickbait that misleads — it tanks watch time
- Use numbers, brackets [], or parentheses () for visual scanning
- A/B test: offer one SEO-forward title and one curiosity title

### TikTok / Instagram Reels
- Title = on-screen text hook (first 1–2 seconds)
- Short and punchy: under 10 words
- Use "POV:", "Tell me why...", "The way I...", "Nobody talks about..."

### LinkedIn
- Conversational opener, then context
- "I [did X]. Here's what I learned:" format works well
- No hashtags in titles — save them for comments

### Twitter/X
- Thread hooks: First tweet must standalone as a hook
- "I [X]. A thread 🧵" or "Hot take: [Statement]" or "[Number] things about [X]:"

## Step 5: Thumbnail Brief

For every YouTube or Instagram post, generate a **thumbnail design brief**:

```
🖼️ THUMBNAIL BRIEF

Concept: [1-sentence description of the visual]
Background: [Color, gradient, scene — high contrast preferred]
Main Subject: [Person, graphic, product — what's the hero element]
Text Overlay: [Short text — 3–5 words max, large and readable]
Color Palette: [2–3 colors — avoid muddy combos]
Emotion / Expression: [If person: surprised, excited, serious, pointing]
Visual Hook: [What makes someone stop scrolling]

Style Reference: [Minimalist / Bold / Clean / Chaotic-energy / etc.]

Do's:
- High contrast between subject and background
- Face close to camera if person is featured (emotions = clicks)
- Text that complements the title, not repeats it
- Rule of thirds for layout

Don'ts:
- Too much text
- Small faces or unrecognizable subjects
- Clashing colors
- Busy backgrounds that compete with the subject
```

---

## Step 6: A/B Testing Guidance

Always offer 2–3 title variations for A/B testing. Explain:
- **Version A**: SEO-optimized (better for search discovery)
- **Version B**: Curiosity-driven (better for browse/suggested)
- **Version C**: Benefit-forward (best for paid promotion or email)

---

## Tips

- **Existing title to improve**: Score it first, then show what's weak and offer improved versions.
- **Thumbnail feedback**: If user shares a thumbnail image, critique it against the brief criteria above.
- **Seasonal/trending titles**: Use web search to check trending angles for the topic.
- **Title + Thumbnail alignment**: The two must work together — the thumbnail teases, the title explains.
